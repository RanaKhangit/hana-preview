Lotus

The flower of rising clear

The Power of the FlowerChosen for its character and lore; part of a personalised botanical ritual.

The lotus draws its whole meaning from where it grows: rooted in dark water, it opens each morning untouched and clean. For three thousand years the East has read that as the image of rising clear. In the HANA garden it is the flower of beginning again, unmarked by where you have been.

The meaning of Lotus

The lotus symbolizes purity, rebirth, and enlightenment across Eastern cultures. Its cycle of daily emergence and closing mirrors spiritual transformation. Ancient Egyptian mythology linked the lotus to the sun god Ra and creation—the flower emerged from primordial waters, symbolizing regeneration and the origins of life. In Hindu and Buddhist traditions, the lotus is central: deities sit upon it, representing the divine flowering of consciousness from murky attachment. The lotus flower represents purity of body, speech, and mind—untouched by the waters from which it rises. The lotus is the national flower of both India and Vietnam.
