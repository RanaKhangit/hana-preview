Passionflower

The flower of contemplative depth

The Power of the FlowerPassionflower is chosen for its character of spiritual depth and contemplative mystery.

Sixteenth-century missionaries read the whole story of the Passion in this one intricate bloom, and contemplative traditions have used it as a doorway to stillness ever since. In the HANA garden it is the flower of touching something quiet and deep.

The meaning of Passionflower

The passionflower's intricate structure was interpreted by 16th-century Christian missionaries as a symbol of Christ's Passion—its 10 petals representing the apostles, the three stigmas representing the nails, the five anthers the Sacred Wounds, and the filament circle the Crown of Thorns. In Hindu tradition, passionflower is believed to symbolise the Five Pandava Brothers. The flower has been used in ritual and meditation across cultures for its association with spiritual depth and contemplative presence. South American Indigenous traditions used it in ceremonial tea practices.
