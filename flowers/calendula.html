Calendula

The flower that turns to the light

The Power of the FlowerCalendula is a flower of resilience, warmth and joyful vitality.

Calendula follows the sun across the sky and closes when it sets, which is why the old apothecaries called it the bride of the sun. From Egypt to the Aztec altar it has carried warmth and a sense of protection. In the HANA garden it is the flower of turning, gently, toward the light.

The meaning of Calendula

Called 'Mary's Gold' by Christian tradition, calendula holds sacred significance across cultures. Ancient Egyptians associated it with sun god Ra, using it in rituals honouring vitality. Greeks and Romans carried it to weddings as a symbol of love and devotion. Medieval European herbalists carried marigold as a protective talisman against plague. In India, calendula remains one of the most sacred flowers, representing gratitude, excellence, and serenity. The Aztecs used it in religious ceremony and protection rituals. Calendula follows the sun across the sky—called 'Bride of the Sun' (Solis Sponsa).
