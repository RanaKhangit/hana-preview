Forget-Me-Not

The flower of faithful memory

The Power of the FlowerForget-me-not is chosen for its character of faithful remembrance and tender loyalty.

A knight, the legend says, threw these to his love as the river took him, calling their name: forget me not. Small and blue and constant, they have meant faithful remembrance ever since. In the HANA garden it is the flower of being held in someone's memory.

The meaning of Forget-Me-Not

Forget-me-not symbolises remembrance, true love, and unwavering loyalty. In Victorian flower language, it signified constancy and eternal devotion, particularly during times of separation. A medieval German legend tells of a knight who, whilst picking flowers for his beloved, fell into the Danube River and threw the bouquet to her with his final cry: 'Vergiss mein nicht!'—Forget me not! During Nazi Germany, Freemasons secretly adopted the flower as a symbol of brotherhood and resistance when their insignia was outlawed. Today, forget-me-not is the official symbol of Alzheimer's awareness—a poetic emblem of memory and cherished connection.
