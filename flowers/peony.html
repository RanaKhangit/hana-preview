The flower of unashamed abundance

The Power of the FlowerThe peony teaches abundance without apology.

The peony does not hold herself back. Where other blooms are modest she opens in great extravagant layers, and for two thousand years the East has read that fullness as a sign of fortune. In the HANA garden the peony is the flower of receiving without apology.

The meaning of Peony

Cultivated in China across many centuries, peonies achieved unmatched status. Called 'the king of flowers,' they symbolized imperial luxury, prosperity, and honor. During the Tang Dynasty, peonies became palace treasures. In Chinese symbolism, they represent wealth, honor, and romantic love—paintings of peonies hung in homes to attract good fortune. In Japanese tradition, peonies symbolize honor, courage, and happy marriage. Greek mythology tells of Paeon, a physician to the gods, transformed into a peony to escape jealousy. Victorians associated peonies with bashfulness and modesty, despite their extravagant blooms.
