Marigold

The flower of golden remembrance

The Power of the FlowerThe marigold is the flower of continuity and golden protection.

Marigold blooms on thresholds: between seasons, between the living and the remembered. From Mary's gold to the Aztec cempasuchil that lights the way home, its warmth has always been both celebration and a sense of protection. In the HANA garden it is the flower of being held by something larger than yourself.

The meaning of Marigold

The name 'marigold' comes from 'Mary's gold,' likely referring to flowers presented to the Virgin Mary when worshippers couldn't afford actual gold. The Aztecs called marigolds 'cempasúchil', believing their bright color and pungent aroma beckoned the souls of the departed. This tradition continues in Day of the Dead celebrations, where marigold petals guide ancestral spirits home. In India, marigolds symbolize the sun, passion, wealth, and happiness, central to weddings and Diwali. Buddhists use golden marigold garlands as temple offerings. Victorian flower language associated marigolds with grief and remembrance.
