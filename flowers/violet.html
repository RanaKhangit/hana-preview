Violet

The flower of quiet faithfulness

The Power of the FlowerThe violet whispers a secret: some of the most powerful things are hidden.

The violet hides low and waits to be found, and for that the ancients made it the flower of modesty and faithful love. In the HANA garden it is the flower of the quiet loyalty that does not need to be seen to be true.

The meaning of Violet

In Greek mythology, violets are said to have sprung from Zeus's tears shed for Io, a nymph he loved. Artemis also transformed a nymph into violets to protect her virginity. Persephone was gathering violets when she was taken by Hades—making them her beloved flower. Violets were planted on graves in medieval Europe, especially of children, believed to comfort the grieving. Associated with the Virgin Mary, violets symbolize humility and modesty. In Victorian flower language, they represented modesty, faithfulness, and everlasting love. The earliest cultivation of violets reaches back into antiquity, with ancient Greeks using them in wine and apothecary lore.

The sensory ritual

As a ritual, Violet belongs to calm, stillness and the gentle easing of the day. A few drops of a Violet-led flower-essence blend, an unhurried breath, a quiet moment held with the soft, settling quiet of its character — a symbolic, sensory pause chosen for what it means and how the ritual feels, not for scent or any effect. A flower essence is not an aromatic oil; Violet is present here for its lore, not its fragrance.
