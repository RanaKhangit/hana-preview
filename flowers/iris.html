Iris

The flower of clear seeing

The Power of the FlowerIris is chosen for its character of clarity and inner knowing.

The iris is named for the rainbow goddess who carried messages between worlds, and from Egypt's sphinx to the French fleur-de-lis it has meant wisdom and regal clarity. In the HANA garden it is the flower of seeing plainly and speaking true.

The meaning of Iris

Named after the Greek goddess Iris, messenger to the gods who travelled the rainbow between heaven and earth. In ancient Egypt, iris was placed on the brow of the Sphinx and the sceptres of pharaohs, symbolising wisdom, faith, and bravery. The flower became fleur-de-lis in French heraldry, a symbol of royalty and persuasive authority. Greek tradition held that planting iris on graves would guide the departed soul to the afterlife.

The sensory ritual

As a ritual, Iris belongs to grace, poise and quiet presence. A few drops of a Iris-led flower-essence blend, an unhurried breath, a quiet moment held with the composed, unhurried poise of its character — a symbolic, sensory pause chosen for what it means and how the ritual feels, not for scent or any effect. A flower essence is not an aromatic oil; Iris is present here for its lore, not its fragrance.
