The coneflower of the open prairie is honoured in Great Plains tradition as a sacred life-plant, its deep purple a sign of sovereignty and resilience. In the HANA garden it is the flower of standing rooted in your own ground.

The meaning of Echinacea

Echinacea is a native prairie wildflower deeply honoured in Great Plains Indigenous traditions across the centuries. Among Western tribes, it is called 'elk root' due to the belief that wounded elk seek it out for restoration. The Navajo people consider coneflower one of the sacred life-plants. In magical traditions, purple echinacea represents sovereignty, power, and spiritual wisdom. Its name comes from Greek 'echinos' (hedgehog), referencing the spiky seed head. Native American lore associates the flower with resilience, strength, and spiritual power.
