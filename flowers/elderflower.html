Elderflower

The flower of quiet sanctuary

The Power of the FlowerA flower for those who wish to embody gentle protection and compassionate presence.

Elderflower grows on a tree that European folklore guarded with its own spirit, the Elder Mother. Its small white blossoms have always meant shelter and gentle renewal. In the HANA garden it is the flower of making sanctuary.

The meaning of Elderflower

In European folklore, the elder tree is sacred, protected by the 'Elder Mother'—a spirit believed to grant blessings and ward off negativity. Norse mythology associates elderflower with Freya, goddess of love and fertility. Celtic tradition honours it as a symbol of renewal and protective magic, often hung above doorways for spiritual protection.

Source: Traditional / lore-based

Elderflower as a HANA flower guide

A flower for those who wish to embody gentle protection and compassionate presence. Elderflower creates ritual space for release and renewal—the feeling of standing under a full moon, safe and seen. This is HANA's reading of the flower's lore, an interpretation rather than a claim about your body or your health.

Source: Brand interpretation

The sensory ritual

As a ritual, Elderflower belongs to lightness, clarity and a sense of renewal. A few drops of a Elderflower-led flower-essence blend, an unhurried breath, a quiet moment held with the cool, luminous lightness of its character — a symbolic, sensory pause chosen for what it means and how the ritual feels, not for scent or any effect. A flower essence is not an aromatic oil; Elderflower is present here for its lore, not its fragrance.

Source: Brand interpretation

When you may choose Elderflower

You may choose Elderflower when your ritual calls for lightness, clarity and a sense of renewal. There is no right reason and no wrong one. Elderflower simply keeps company with the part of you that is ready to begin again.
