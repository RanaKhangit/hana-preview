Orange Blossom

The flower of fresh beginnings

The Power of the FlowerA flower for transitions and fresh starts.

Orange blossom appears on the branch at the same time as the fruit, flowering and bearing at once. Persian and Victorian brides wore it for exactly that promise of beginning. In the HANA garden it is the flower of beginning again, lightly.

The meaning of Orange Blossom

Orange blossom has been a symbol of purity, fertility, and eternal love across cultures for millennia. In Persian wedding ceremonies, brides wear orange blossom crowns to ensure blessings and longevity. Queen Victoria popularised the Victorian tradition of orange blossom in bridal wear (1840), establishing it as a symbol of happiness and new beginnings. Chinese tradition honours the tree for simultaneously bearing fruit and flowers—a symbol of abundance and generative power.

The sensory ritual

As a ritual, Orange Blossom belongs to lightness, clarity and a sense of renewal. A few drops of a Orange-led flower-essence blend, an unhurried breath, a quiet moment held with the cool, luminous lightness of its character — a symbolic, sensory pause chosen for what it means and how the ritual feels, not for scent or any effect. A flower essence is not an aromatic oil; Orange Blossom is present here for its lore, not its fragrance.
