Jasmine

The flower of the senses at night

The Power of the FlowerJasmine invites you into the night of your own senses.

Jasmine waits for the dark to release its scent, and Persia named it a gift from God for exactly that nocturnal depth. In the HANA garden it is the flower of trusting your own senses and your own quiet magnetism.

The meaning of Jasmine

The name is Persian, meaning 'a gift from God.' Jasmine has deep roots in ancient Egypt, China, and Persia, revered for its treasured qualities and beauty. In Hindu mythology, jasmine is linked to Lakshmi, goddess of wealth and prosperity, and is one of the five arrows of Kamadeva, the god of love. In Buddhism, jasmine symbolizes purity of mind and the attainment of nirvana. Islamic tradition holds jasmine as sacred, held sacred and linked with the soul and with inner peace. Chinese tradition celebrates jasmine tea, associating the flower with sensuality, love, and good fortune.

The sensory ritual

As a ritual, Jasmine belongs to brightness, play and a lift in mood. A few drops of a Jasmine-led flower-essence blend, an unhurried breath, a quiet moment held with the bright, open lift of its character — a symbolic, sensory pause chosen for what it means and how the ritual feels, not for scent or any effect. A flower essence is not an aromatic oil; Jasmine is present here for its lore, not its fragrance.
